Basic Gameplay

BlackLash plays like a classic 1990s arcade shooter. The player moves left and right along the bottom of the screen and fires upward to destroy waves of enemies. The longer you survive, the faster and denser the enemies become, creating a sense of chaos and urgency.

Enemies and movement

Enemies drop from the top of the screen in lanes. They represent figures of power and racism: police, judges, KKK members, and Nazis. Some enemies split into multiple smaller ones when shot, making it harder to stay alive and showing how hate or bias can multiply instead of disappearing.

Avatars and roles

Before playing, you choose from four avatars, each based on exaggerated racial stereotypes. Mongrel used these characters to make players question how games historically portrayed Black identity and who gets to be the “default” hero.

Visuals and sound

The graphics mix pixel art, collage, and sampled images. The soundtrack borrows snippets of hip-hop and computer noises. Together they give the game a raw, hacked feeling, half protest and half glitch, highlighting that this isn’t about polish, but about message.
